ROUTINE

Morning (7 am - 9 am):

  1. Wake up and Hydrate: Drink a glass of water as soon as you wake up to rehydrate your body.
  2. Morning Exercise: Engage in a 30-minute exercise routine to get your body and mind active. This can help improve focus and memory.
  3. Healthy Breakfast: Have a nutritious breakfast to fuel your body for the day ahead.
  4. Planning Time: Take 10-15 minutes to review your goals, including your learning objectives for the new skill, and tasks for the day. Use a planner or digital tool to organize your schedule.

Late Morning to Afternoon (9 am - 12 pm):

  1. Focused Skill Development: Dedicate at least 2 hours to learning the new skill. Break it down into smaller, manageable tasks. Use online courses, tutorials, or books to guide your learning process. Take short breaks to rest and process the information.

Midday (12 pm - 1 pm):

  1. Lunch Break: Take a well-deserved break and have a nutritious lunch.

Afternoon (1 pm - 4 pm):

  1. Spaced Repetition Practice: Spend 1-2 hours reviewing and practicing what you've learned in the morning session. Spaced repetition, which involves revisiting the material at increasing intervals, enhances memory retention.
  2. Apply the Skill: Use the newly acquired knowledge practically. If applicable, work on small projects or exercises related to the skill you're learning.

Late Afternoon (4 pm - 6 pm):

  1. Physical Activity/Outdoor Time: Engage in physical activity or go outdoors to refresh your mind. Physical activity can stimulate creativity and help consolidate learning

Evening (6 pm - 8 pm):

  1. Dinner: Have a healthy and balanced dinner.
  2. Personal Development: Use this time for personal development activities, like journaling about your progress and insights, or researching additional resources to enhance your learning.

Night (8 pm - 10 pm):

  1. Review and Summary: Allocate 30 minutes to review what you've learned throughout the day. Summarize key concepts and take notes for future reference.
  2. Relaxation Time: Spend time doing activities that help you unwind and relax, such as listening to music, reading, or spending time with loved ones.

Bedtime (10 pm):

  1. Sleep: Aim to get 7-8 hours of quality sleep to consolidate learning and allow your brain to process information effectively
